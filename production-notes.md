(1) I chose to go with the first edition of this book. Later editions were substantially different: changing the names of characters, the title of the book, and making significant plot changes. See https://www.persee.fr/doc/cchav_0184-1025_2002_num_32_1_1334 for some notes on the various editions.

(2) The PG transcription omitted the author's autobiographical introduction; I have restored this.

(3) The original usually uses "negro" uncapitalized; PG corrected this to "Negro"; I reverted this to the original.

(4) The original smooshes a lot of back-and-forth dialog into single paragraphs; I've untangled this into one-paragraph-per-speaker in the modern style.

(5) In Chapter 12, a character's pronunciation of the word hair is confusingly rendered as "'hair'" in one place, "'hare'" in another; I am guessing that the intention of the author was "har" (a later edition supports this guess), which is how he renders it in another chapter, and I have altered the chapter to use "har".
